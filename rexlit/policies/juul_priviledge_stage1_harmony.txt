# Policy

## 1) Definitions

### 1.1 Attorney-Client Privilege (ACP)
Confidential communications between a client and their lawyer (or representatives) made **in confidence** and **for the purpose of obtaining or providing legal advice**.

### 1.2 Work Product (WP)
Documents, materials, or tangible things **prepared by or for counsel in anticipation of litigation or for trial**, including (non-exhaustive): attorney notes/memoranda, legal research/analysis, litigation strategy documents, and expert preparation materials.

### 1.3 Common Interest (CI)
ACP or WP **shared among multiple parties** who have a **common legal interest** **pursuant to an executed common-interest agreement**, disclosed **to advance a shared legal strategy** (a mere business interest is insufficient).

---

## 2) Privileged Indicators (weigh strongly toward privilege)

2.1 **Attorney Identifiers**
- External law-firm domains (e.g., *@lawfirm.com*)
- In-house counsel mailboxes (e.g., *@company.com* accounts titled “legal”, “counsel”, etc.)
- Metadata/name cues (e.g., “Esq.”, “Attorney at Law”, bar numbers)

2.2 **Explicit Markers**
- “Attorney-client privilege”, “Privileged and confidential”, “Attorney work product”, “Work product—do not distribute”, “Common interest”

2.3 **Legal Advice Language**
- Clear indications that legal advice, opinions, or legal analysis is being sought, provided, or discussed

2.4 **Litigation Preparation**
- Draft pleadings/motions, litigation or settlement strategy, deposition prep, expert strategy, similar materials tied to litigation

2.5 **Contextual Metadata**
- Authorized counsel lists, matter titles, litigation hold references, or equivalent metadata tying content to legal advice or litigation preparation

---

## 3) Non-Privileged (weigh against privilege)

3.1 **Routine Business**
Operational, sales, marketing, product, or general business discussions without a legal-advice component

3.2 **Public Information**
Public documents, general knowledge, marketing materials

3.3 **Attorney Acting in Business Capacity**
Counsel giving business—not legal—advice; attorneys acting as executives/board members without a legal purpose

3.4 **No Expectation of Confidentiality**
Broad distribution outside the privilege circle; public announcements; communications with third parties lacking a valid CI agreement

3.5 **Mere CC to Counsel**
Counsel copied “for awareness” without a legal request or advice

---

## 4) Edge Cases & Decision Notes

4.1 **Mixed Content**
If a substantial portion is privileged, classify as privileged; reflect uncertainty in the confidence score.

4.2 **Attorney in Dual Roles**
When attorneys wear business hats, do **not** presume privilege; require an explicit legal-advice purpose (see §1.1).

4.3 **Distribution Scope**
Privilege generally requires a confined audience (client+counsel+agents or valid CI circle). Wide circulation weighs against privilege.

4.4 **Forwarding**
Forwarding privileged content outside the privilege or CI circle may waive privilege; evaluate recipients and purpose.

---

## 5) Confidence Scoring Heuristics

- **0.90–1.00**: Explicit markers + attorney identifiers + clear legal-advice or litigation-prep context
- **0.75–0.89**: Strong signals (e.g., attorney identifiers + legal-advice request/provision)
- **0.50–0.74**: Moderate signals (e.g., attorney included but role unclear; some legal terminology)
- **0.25–0.49**: Weak signals (e.g., legal terms used generically; no attorney participation)
- **0.00–0.24**: No indicators or clearly non-privileged per §3

---

## 6) Non-binding Scenarios (no excerpts; for orientation only)

- **Clear ACP**: External counsel communicating legal opinion to client regarding a transaction → likely **PRIVILEGED:ACP** (see §§1.1, 2.1–2.3).
- **Work Product**: In-house counsel memo titled/marked as attorney work product discussing litigation strategy → likely **PRIVILEGED:WP** (see §§1.2, 2.2, 2.4).
- **Routine Business**: Internal email on quarterly sales with no attorney involvement → **Non-privileged** (see §3.1).
- **Attorney CC’d**: Product launch planning with GC only copied for awareness → **Non-privileged** (see §3.5).
- **Attorney as Business Advisor**: Counsel discusses business strategy without specific legal issue → **Non-privileged** (see §3.3).
- **Common Interest**: Counsel message among aligned parties referencing executed CI agreement and joint litigation strategy → **PRIVILEGED:CI** (see §§1.3, 2.2, 2.4).
